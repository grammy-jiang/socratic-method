# Idea brief: New hire mentor pairing

## What changed under questioning
Initial thesis: pair every new hire with a senior for their first quarter, weekly 1:1s, aimed at onboarding support. Under questioning, the concrete problem became clearer (no structure, no map of who to ask, hires cold-DM'ing people and feeling bad about it) — but two load-bearing assumptions surfaced as untested: whether a mentor relationship (vs. documentation) is what actually fixes this, and whether seniors have the capacity to do it well.

## Scope
A 30-person org; every new hire; weekly 1:1s with an assigned senior for the first quarter. Exact format still open.

## Assumptions surfaced
The user has not compared a mentor relationship against alternatives like better onboarding docs or a Slack channel guide, and does not know what the mentor relationship provides beyond "a person." The user also does not know whether the seniors who'd take this on have the time to do it properly.

## Contradictions & how resolved
None surfaced yet.

## Open questions (aporia)
- Would documentation alone close the same gaps, or is the mentor relationship doing something docs can't?
- Do the seniors have real bandwidth for this, or would it compete with their existing workload?
